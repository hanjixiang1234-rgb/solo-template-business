## Product Strategy

### Core audience

Primary audience:
- China-based freelancers
- solo operators
- consultants
- service sellers
- people trying to use AI to organize work and make money online

What they want:
- practical AI workflows
- simple systems they can copy
- less chaos in client work
- useful templates instead of vague theory

### Account-to-offer positioning

The account is the traffic engine.

The products are support tools for the audience that content attracts:
- templates
- checklists
- mini playbooks
- trackers
- operating guides

### Offer ladder

1. Free checklist
- Purpose: capture interest and validate demand
- Example: one-person business setup checklist

2. Low-ticket starter pack
- Target price: RMB 19 to 39
- Purpose: first conversion
- Example: client proposal and onboarding starter kit

3. Core template bundle
- Target price: RMB 69 to 129
- Purpose: main offer
- Example: freelancer back-office system

4. Expanded playbook bundle
- Target price: RMB 129 to 199
- Purpose: higher-value upsell
- Example: AI plus templates plus workflow guide

### Packaging rules

- Every asset must solve one operational problem.
- Every product must include examples and instructions.
- Every product should connect directly to a proven content topic.
- The account should teach first and sell second.

### Pricing logic

- Keep the first offer cheap and easy to understand.
- Increase price only after demand is validated.
- Bundle only after two or more standalone assets are proven.

### Expansion path

Phase 1:
- Xiaohongshu only

Phase 2:
- Add a second content channel only if the first workflow is stable

Phase 3:
- Build a lightweight storefront or delivery page for offers
